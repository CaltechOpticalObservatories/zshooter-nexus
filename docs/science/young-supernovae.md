# Young Supernovae

```{container} zs-lead
Spectra obtained during the first hours and days after explosion measure short-lived emission from circumstellar material and the outer ejecta.
```

The short-lived lines identify material lost by the progenitor shortly before explosion. This connects the early spectrum to the final years of stellar mass loss.

## Instrument Requirements

- The 0.31 to 2.45 um range includes blue O and He lines, optical H and He, and He I features in K band.
- R > 10,000 is used to separate narrow components and constrain winds of 10 to 50 km/s for brighter targets.

## Observing Requirements

- For events within ≈ 50 Mpc, the source can be fainter than 23 AB during the period when flash-ionized lines are present.
- The baseline requirement is S/N = 5 at ug ≈ 23 and R ≈ 3,000 in one hour.
- Observations are required within 15 minutes. Three-band imaging supports photometric calibration and continued monitoring.
