# Relativistic Explosions

```{container} zs-lead
GRB afterglows and luminous fast blue optical transients require rapid low-resolution spectroscopy while the source remains observable.
```

Early spectra determine redshift and classification while the continuum is still bright. They also measure absorption and line changes before the source fades.

## Instrument Requirements

- Simultaneous 0.31 to 2.45 um coverage provides redshifts, continuum shape, and absorption diagnostics, including high-redshift GRB afterglows.
- Three-band imaging provides detections, colors, or limits when spectroscopy of the transient is no longer feasible.

## Observing Requirements

- Reference targets are u ≈ 24.5 for GRB afterglows and r ≈ 24 for luminous fast blue optical transients, at S/N = 5 after binning to R ≈ 1,000.
- The required response is within five minutes, with individual exposures no longer than ≈ 10 minutes.
